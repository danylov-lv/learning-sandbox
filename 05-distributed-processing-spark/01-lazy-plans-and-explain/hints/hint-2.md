**Job counting.** `spark.sparkContext.statusTracker()` gives you a live view into what the application has actually run. `getJobIdsForGroup()` called with no argument returns every job id the whole `SparkContext` has run since it started — its length is a running total. Read that length, do something, read it again; the delta tells you how many jobs that something triggered. Watch out for one thing before you build your baseline: `spark.read.json(some_dir)` without an explicit schema makes Spark sample the data to infer column types, and that sampling is itself a Spark job. If you take your "zero jobs so far" baseline before the read, that inference job will look like it came from your transformations, which is not what you're demonstrating. Take the baseline right after the read.

**Narrow vs wide.** A narrow dependency means each output partition depends on a known, small set of input partitions — Spark can compute it without moving data between partitions. `filter` and `select` are narrow. A wide dependency means the output depends on data that could be anywhere across all input partitions — `groupBy(...).agg(...)`, `distinct()`, and most joins are wide, because rows with the same key have to end up together, and they started out scattered arbitrarily across partitions. Wide dependencies are exactly what force a shuffle, and a shuffle is exactly what shows up as an `Exchange` node in the physical plan. So: build one query that never needs same-key rows to meet, and one that does, and read their plans.

**JSONL vs Parquet.** Don't guess what "pushdown" and "pruning" look like in the plan text — look. Call `harness.common.get_plan(df)` on both sides of your comparison and read the two strings printed out (temporarily, while developing — a `print()` in your own scratch script, not in the final `explore.py`). Look specifically at the line that starts with `Batched:` and the line that starts with `PushedFilters:` on the `Scan` node. They will not say what you might expect from a purely "predicate pushdown = filters not shown at all" mental model — both formats will list `PushedFilters`. The real, load-bearing difference is elsewhere.
